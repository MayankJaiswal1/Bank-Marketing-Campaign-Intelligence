Section 06

What to do with this

  • 01

    Re-engage before you cold-call

    Clients with a successful prior campaign convert at 65%, versus 8.8% with no history. Build and maintain a warm-lead list rather than defaulting to fresh contacts.

  • 02

    Shift volume out of May–August

    March, September, October, and December convert 4–5× higher than the high-volume summer months. Reallocate calling capacity toward the shoulder months where feasible.

  • 03

    Weight outreach toward students, retirees, and 66+ clients

    These segments convert at 2–4× the blue-collar/services baseline. They warrant tailored scripts, not just tailored targeting.

  • 04

    Track the macro backdrop, not just the client list

    Euribor rate, employment level, and employment variation are the three strongest predictors in the model — stronger than any single client attribute. Campaign timing relative to the rate cycle deserves its own review.

  • 05

    Use the model to rank, not to gatekeep

    At 42% precision / 64% recall, the Random Forest is a prioritization tool for a call list — not a filter that should exclude anyone outright.
